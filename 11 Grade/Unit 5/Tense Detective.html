Telling Stories: Past Simple vs. Past Continuous

When we tell stories about discoveries, we usually need two tenses. They work together like a movie camera: one sets the scene, and the other captures the sudden action.

🎥 Past Continuous - The Background

Form: was / were + verb + -ing

Use: For an action already in progress. It sets the scene or describes a longer background action.

✏️In 1935, a reporter was writing a story. It was raining outside.
⚡ Past Simple - The Interruption

Form: regular verbs end in -ed (invented), irregular verbs change form (got, saw, wrote).

Use: For a finished action, or a shorter, sudden action that interrupts the background scene.

🍟George Crum invented crisps in 1853. Suddenly, the pen ran out of ink.

While & When - Connecting Tenses

Use while and when to connect the two tenses in one sentence.

⌛While I was reading (long action), the phone rang (short action).
📞I was reading (long action) when the phone rang (short action).
🖊 Real Story: While Bíró was writing his article, he got annoyed with his pen. This led him to invent the ballpoint pen!

💡 Quick Reference Guide

Use these clues to identify which tense to use!

⌛ Past Continuous Clues
🔑
Key words: while, when (before a long action), at that moment, all day, all night
🎬
Think: the "background movie" - the action was already playing when something else happened.
✅
Bíró was working as a journalist when he noticed the ink dried quickly.
⚡ Past Simple Clues
🔑
Key words: when (before a short action), suddenly, then, after that, in [year], one day, finally
📸
Think: the "snapshot" - a completed action with a clear beginning and end.
✅
George Crum invented the potato crisp in 1853.

🧠 Memory Tricks

🎥
The Movie Trick: Past Continuous = film playing in the background. Past Simple = someone walks in and interrupts the film.
🌊
The River Trick: Past Continuous = flowing river (ongoing). Past Simple = stone dropped in (sudden event).
🔤
WHILE = -ing: If you see "while", the verb after it is almost always Past Continuous (was/were + -ing).
⚡
SUDDENLY = Past Simple: If you see "suddenly", the verb is almost always Past Simple.

📝 Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌
Wrong: While he was working, he was inventing the pen.
Why: Two ongoing actions with "while" - the invention was a sudden, complete event.
✅
Right: While he was working, he invented the pen.
