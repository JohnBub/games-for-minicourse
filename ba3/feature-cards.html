CONSONANT FEATURES
C1 TH-Realisation (/θ/ and /ð/)
👁 What to Spot
Look at words with ⟨th⟩: the, this, that, three, think, with. In RP they are [θ] (voiceless) or [ð] (voiced). Any other realisation = diagnostic.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ [t̪ d̪] (dental stops, tongue ON teeth) → IrE
→ [f v] (labiodental fricatives) → ScotE or Broad AusE
→ [t d] (alveolar stops, plain) → NigE SingE AAVE
→ [tʰ] (aspirated stop) → IndE
→ [θ ð] (standard) → RP SAE
📝 Examples
three: RP [θɹiː] → IrE [t̪ɹiː] → NigE [tri] → IndE [tʰri]
the: RP [ðə] → IrE [d̪ə] → ScotE [və]
⚠️ Trap Don't confuse [t̪] (dental = IrE) with [t] (alveolar = NigE/AAVE). The subscript dental diacritic [ ̪] under the symbol is the clue. Also: IrE dental [t̪] is NOT aspirated; IndE [tʰ] IS.
Puskás Ch.3:3 (IrE/ScotE), Ch.6 (NigE), Ch.7 (IndE)
C2 Rhoticity (post-vocalic /r/)
👁 What to Spot
Look at /r/ after vowels — words like car, bird, letter, park, more. Is /r/ pronounced or silent?
🌳 Decision Tree
→ /r/ IS pronounced after vowels (RHOTIC):
IrE ScotE SAE IndE
→ /r/ is NOT pronounced (NON-RHOTIC):
RP GAusE AAVE NigE SingE
📝 Examples
car: SAE [kʰɑɹ] vs RP [kʰɑː] vs GAusE [kʰɑː]
letter: IrE [lɛtɚ] (rhotic) vs NigE [lɛta] (no /r/, no schwa)
R-type also matters: IrE [ɻ] retroflex · ScotE [ɾ] tap · SAE [ɹ] approximant
⚠️ Trap Rhoticity alone doesn't ID the variety — 4 varieties are rhotic! Combine with other features. Also: AAVE is non-rhotic but can have /r/ in onset (run [ɹʌn]) — only POST-VOCALIC /r/ counts.
Puskás Ch.3:1 (IrE/ScotE), Ch.4 (SAE/GAusE)
C3 /l/ Quality (Clear vs Dark vs Vocalized)
👁 What to Spot
Check all /l/ positions. Look for: [l] (clear), [lˠ] or [ɫ] (dark/velarized), or vowel-like [ʊ]/[o] replacing /l/ (vocalized).
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Clear [l] in ALL positions → IrE (except Belfast)
→ Dark [ɫ] in ALL positions → ScotE
→ Clear onset / Dark coda → RP SAE GAusE (standard split)
→ /l/ → vowel [ʊ] (vocalized) → AAVE
📝 Examples
also: IrE [ɑlso] (clear) vs ScotE [ɑɫso] (dark) vs RP [ɔːɫsəʊ] (dark in coda)
help: AAVE [hɛʊp] (vocalized) vs RP [hɛɫp]
⚠️ Trap The midterm model showed IrE speaker from Strabane (near Belfast) using dark /l/ before consonants — Northern Irish can break the rule! Always note if distribution is conditioned (before consonants only) vs categorical (everywhere).
Puskás Ch.3:3 (IrE/ScotE), Ch.4:4 (GAusE); Midterm model §5
C4 H-Dropping
👁 What to Spot
Words starting with ⟨h⟩: her, him, house, have, happy. Is [h] present or absent in initial position?
🌳 Decision Tree
→ [h] ALWAYS dropped → GAusE
→ [h] sometimes dropped → IrE (sporadic) · urban BrE (non-RP)
→ [h] always present → RP SAE ScotE and most others
📝 Examples
her: GAusE [ɜ] or [ɚ] (always dropped) vs IrE [hɚ] (mostly kept) vs RP [hɜː]
house: GAusE [æʊs] vs RP [haʊs]
⚠️ Trap In the midterm, the IrE speaker dropped /h/ once out of many tokens — this is sporadic, not regular. If /h/ is dropped every time = GAusE. Sporadic = possible IrE or ongoing change. Also: function words (her, him, have) drop /h/ more easily than content words.
Puskás Ch.4:4 (GAusE); Trudgill 2019 (IrE/ScotE)
C5 Aspiration of Voiceless Plosives
👁 What to Spot
Check voiceless plosives /p t k/ in onset. Is [ʰ] (puff of air) present? Look for [pʰ tʰ kʰ] vs [p t k].
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Aspirated [pʰ tʰ kʰ] in onset → RP SAE GAusE AAVE
→ NO aspiration [p t k] → IrE ScotE NigE IndE SingE
📝 Examples
park: RP [pʰɑːk] vs NigE [pak] (no aspiration, no length)
call: SAE [kʰɑːl] vs IrE [kɑl]
⚠️ Trap Lack of aspiration in NigE is due to substrate influence (West African languages don't aspirate). In IrE/ScotE it's a Celtic feature. Same surface feature, different cause — mention the substrate in your analysis!
Puskás Ch.3 (IrE/ScotE), Ch.6 (NigE substrate)
C6 Consonant Cluster Simplification
👁 What to Spot
Final consonant clusters reduced: post → pos, hand → han, cold → col. Missing final consonants in clusters.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Systematic/heavy cluster reduction → NigE (substrate: W. African langs lack complex codas)
→ Variable cluster reduction → AAVE SingE
→ Clusters preserved → RP SAE and most Inner Circle
📝 Examples
NigE: post [pos] · hand [han] · desk [des]
AAVE: cold [koʊl] · test [tɛs]
⚠️ Trap All English speakers simplify clusters sometimes (fast speech). The diagnostic is whether it's systematic (NigE = almost always) vs variable (AAVE = context-dependent). In NigE, reason is substrate (West African phonotactics). In AAVE, also related to morpheme boundaries.
Puskás Ch.5 (AAVE), Ch.6 (NigE)
C7 Intervocalic /t/ (Flapping)
👁 What to Spot
/t/ between two vowels: water, better, city, butter. Does it stay as [t], become a flap [ɾ], or glottal stop [ʔ]?
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Flap [ɾ] → SAE GAusE (also AAVE)
→ Retroflex [ʈ] → IndE
→ Glottal [ʔ] → SingE / urban BrE
→ Plain [t] → RP IrE ScotE
📝 Examples
water: SAE [wɑɾɚ] vs RP [wɔːtə] vs IndE [wɔːʈɚ]
better: GAusE [bɛɾə] vs SingE [bɛʔə]
⚠️ Trap The flap [ɾ] makes SAE writer/rider sound the same (neutralised). This is a shared SAE + GAusE feature — use other clues to distinguish (rhoticity, diphthongs).
Puskás Ch.4
C8 Retroflex Consonants
👁 What to Spot
[ʈ] and [ɖ] symbols — tongue curled back behind alveolar ridge. Instant identifier.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ [ʈ ɖ] for /t d/ → IndE (almost unique marker)
→ [ɻ] retroflex /r/ → IrE
📝 Examples
today: IndE [ʈuɖeː] vs RP [tʰədeɪ]
doctor: IndE [ɖɔkʈɚ]
⚠️ Trap Don't confuse retroflex [ʈ ɖ] (IndE, for ALL /t d/) with dental [t̪ d̪] (IrE, only for TH). Check: is it replacing /t d/ everywhere? → IndE. Only in TH-words? → IrE.

VOWEL FEATURES
V1 KIT-FLEECE Merger (no length contrast)
👁 What to Spot
sit and seat pronounced the same. No [ɪ] vs [iː] distinction — both become [i].
🌳 Decision Tree
→ KIT = FLEECE (merged) → NigE SingE
→ KIT ≠ FLEECE (distinct) → all Inner Circle varieties
Then: downdrift + reduplication → NigE · particles → SingE
📝 Examples
NigE/SingE: sit = seat [sit] · ship = sheep [ʃip]
RP: sit [sɪt] ≠ seat [siːt]
⚠️ Trap Absence of length contrast means NO [ː] diacritic anywhere — check all vowels, not just this pair. If FOOT=GOOSE too, it confirms the pattern. Cause: substrate languages have no length distinction.
Puskás Ch.6 (NigE), Ch.8 (SingE)
V2 FACE/GOAT Monophthongisation
👁 What to Spot
FACE [eɪ] → [eː] and GOAT [əʊ] → [oː]. Diphthongs become long monophthongs.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Both FACE and GOAT as monophthongs → IrE ScotE NigE
Then: rhotic + dental TH → IrE · rhotic + dark /l/ → ScotE · no schwa → NigE
→ Standard diphthongs → RP SAE
→ WIDE diphthongs (opener start) → GAusE (see V5)
📝 Examples
face: RP [feɪs] → IrE [feːs] → GAusE [fæɪs]
goat: RP [gəʊt] → ScotE [goːt] → NigE [got] (short, no length)
⚠️ Trap NigE also monophthongises but the vowel is SHORT [e] not long [eː] — no length contrast in NigE! IrE/ScotE keep the length. Also: Northern BrE monophthongises too.
Puskás Ch.3 (IrE/ScotE), Ch.6 (NigE)
V3 BATH Vowel
👁 What to Spot
Words: bath, staff, ask, class, dance, path. What vowel is used? This is the classic North/South and SAE/RP split.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ [ɑː] (long, back) → RP (Southern BrE) GAusE
→ [æ] (short, front) → SAE + Northern BrE
→ [a] (open front) → IrE ScotE NigE
📝 Examples
bath: RP [bɑːθ] vs SAE [bæθ] vs NigE [bat]
ask: RP [ɑːsk] vs SAE [æsk]
⚠️ Trap BATH=TRAP in SAE and Northern BrE (both [æ]). BATH=TRAP in NigE too but as [a]. The BATH-TRAP split only exists in RP/Southern BrE. Don't assume [æ] = American — Northern English speakers do it too.
Puskás Ch.2 (BrE North/South), Ch.4 (SAE)
V4 PRICE Monophthongisation
👁 What to Spot
PRICE [aɪ] → [aː]. The diphthong becomes a long monophthong. mine sounds like maan.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ PRICE = [aː] monophthong → AAVE (UNIQUE — no other variety does this!)
→ PRICE = [aɪ] diphthong → all other varieties
📝 Examples
AAVE: mine [maːn] · time [taːm] · ride [ɹaːd]
RP: mine [maɪn] · time [taɪm]
⚠️ Trap This is a killer feature — if you see PRICE as [aː], it's AAVE, full stop. Prof. Puskás: "AAVE is the only variety we encountered which has an A instead of an I in PRICE." Instant identification.
Puskás Ch.5 (AAVE)
V5 Wide Diphthongs (Australian Shift)
👁 What to Spot
Diphthongs with a more OPEN starting point than RP. FACE [æɪ] instead of [eɪ]. MOUTH [æʊ] instead of [aʊ].
🌳 Decision Tree
→ FACE = [æɪ] (wider start) → GAusE
→ MOUTH = [æʊ] (fronted start) → GAusE
→ Also: BE long vowels → diphthongs ([iː]→[ɘɪ], [uː]→[ʉu])
📝 Examples
face/mate: GAusE [fæɪs] · [mæɪt] (the iconic "Australian" sound)
please: GAusE [pʰlɘɪz] (FLEECE → diphthong)
snow: GAusE [ʃnɵ̃ʊ] (GOAT → closing diphthong)
⚠️ Trap The FACE vowel [æɪ] in GAusE is the stereotypical marker. Don't confuse GAusE diphthongisation (makes MORE diphthongs from long vowels) with IrE/ScotE/NigE monophthongisation (makes FEWER diphthongs). They go in opposite directions!
Puskás Ch.4:3 (GAusE); Midterm model §1
V6 Schwa & Vowel Reduction
👁 What to Spot
Are unstressed syllables reduced to [ə]? Or does every syllable keep a full vowel? This is the single most powerful filter.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Schwa [ə] present in weak syllables → STRESS-TIMED:
RP IrE ScotE SAE GAusE AAVE
→ NO schwa, all syllables full vowels → SYLLABLE-TIMED:
NigE IndE SingE
📝 Examples
letter: RP [lɛtə] (schwa) vs NigE [lɛta] or [lɛte] (full vowel)
banana: RP [bənɑːnə] (two schwas) vs IndE [banana] (all full)
⚠️ Trap This should be your FIRST FILTER (Step 2 on the one-pager). It instantly splits 9 varieties into two groups. No schwa = Outer Circle (NigE/IndE/SingE). Then use consonants and syntax to distinguish within each group.

V7 STRUT-FOOT Split (or lack of)
👁 What to Spot
cup/put — same vowel or different? RP has [ʌ] vs [ʊ]. Northern BrE has [ʊ] for both.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ STRUT = FOOT (both [ʊ]) → Northern BrE
→ STRUT ≠ FOOT (distinct) → RP (Southern BrE) + most others
📝 Examples
Northern BrE: cup = put [kʊp] = [pʊt]
RP: cup [kʌp] ≠ put [pʊt]
⚠️ Trap This is specifically a British North/South diagnostic. Combined with BATH vowel (V3), it helps pinpoint regional BrE: STRUT=FOOT + BATH=[a] → Northern. STRUT≠FOOT + BATH=[ɑː] → Southern/RP.
Puskás Ch.2 (BrE regional)
V8 FOOT-GOOSE Merger
👁 What to Spot
foot and food pronounced the same. No [ʊ] vs [uː] distinction.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ FOOT = GOOSE (merged) → NigE SingE
→ FOOT ≠ GOOSE → all Inner Circle varieties
Combines with V1 (KIT=FLEECE) to confirm: no length contrast system-wide
📝 Examples
NigE: foot = food [fut] · pull = pool [pul]
RP: foot [fʊt] ≠ food [fuːd]
⚠️ Trap If you see KIT=FLEECE AND FOOT=GOOSE → the whole length system is collapsed. This confirms Outer Circle (NigE or SingE). It's a system-wide pattern, not just individual mergers.
Puskás Ch.6 (NigE), Ch.8 (SingE)
PROSODY & RHYTHM
P1 Stress-Timed vs Syllable-Timed Rhythm
👁 What to Spot
Listen for equal syllable weight (syllable-timed) vs strong/weak alternation (stress-timed). In transcription: check for schwa [ə] and reduced vowels as proxy.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Stress-timed (schwas, reduced syllables):
RP IrE ScotE SAE GAusE AAVE
→ Syllable-timed (all syllables equal, full vowels):
NigE IndE SingE
📝 Examples
Stress-timed: comfortable RP [kʌmftəbəl] (3 syllables spoken, 2 schwas)
Syllable-timed: comfortable IndE [kɔmfɔrtebl] (all 4 syllables pronounced equally)
Prof: "like French" — each syllable has the same weight
⚠️ Trap On a written IPA transcription, you can't directly "hear" rhythm — but you CAN detect it through vowel reduction. No schwa + full vowels everywhere = syllable-timed. This is Card V6's diagnostic applied to rhythm.
Puskás Ch.6-8 (Outer Circle varieties)
P2 Downdrift Intonation
👁 What to Spot
Progressive pitch lowering: H→L→H(lower)→L→H(even lower). Not a continuous fall — resets at phrase boundaries but each peak is lower.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Downdrift pattern → NigE (West African substrate)
→ Particularly salient in questions — starts very high, descends
→ Combined with syllable-timing = strong NigE indicator
📝 Examples
"What makes you so interested?" — starts very high, each subsequent stressed syllable is lower than the previous high point. Prof: "You start relatively high and you lower... high → low → high (lower than first) → low"
⚠️ Trap Downdrift may not be visible in IPA transcription (it's suprasegmental). But if the exam gives audio or asks about intonation patterns, this is unique to NigE in the course. The cause is tonal substrate languages (Yoruba, Igbo).

MORPHOSYNTAX FEATURES
M1 Copula Deletion
👁 What to Spot
Missing is/are: "She Ø nice" or "They Ø happy". The copula (be verb) is absent.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Copula deleted = CURRENT STATE → AAVE
AAVE rule: deleted where StE would CONTRACT (she's → she Ø)
→ Copula deleted (general) → SingE
→ Copula always present → all Inner Circle + NigE/IndE
📝 Examples
AAVE: "She Ø nice" = she is nice RIGHT NOW (temporary state)
vs AAVE: "She be nice" = she is ALWAYS nice (habitual — see M2)
SingE: "This one Ø very good"
⚠️ Trap In AAVE, copula deletion is NOT random — it follows the contraction rule. Where StE can contract (she's), AAVE can delete. Where StE can't contract (*"I know who she's"), AAVE can't delete either. This is systematic, not "lazy speech."
Puskás Ch.5 (AAVE TAM system)
M2 Habitual Markers
👁 What to Spot
Special markers for habitual/ongoing states: "She be working" or "He do be singing".
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Invariant be + V-ing (habitual) → AAVE
→ do be (habitual) → IrE (Northern)
→ be (habitual, without do) → IrE (Southern)
Possible Celtic substrate connection between IrE and AAVE
📝 Examples
AAVE: "She be working late" = she regularly/habitually works late
IrE: "He does be singing all the time" = habitual
Compare: "She working late" (no be) = progressive, happening now
⚠️ Trap Don't confuse AAVE habitual be with Standard English be. AAVE "She be nice" ≠ StE "She is nice." AAVE be = ALWAYS, intrinsic. Copula Ø = RIGHT NOW. This is a rich aspectual distinction that StE lacks.
Puskás Ch.5 (AAVE), Ch.3 (IrE)
M3 AAVE Tense-Aspect-Mood System
👁 What to Spot
Unique aspect markers: done, béen (stressed), finna, steady, come. These are found ONLY in AAVE.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ done + V = completive ("She done left") → AAVE
→ béen (stressed) = remote past ("I béen knew") → AAVE
→ finna = immediate future ("I finna go") → AAVE
→ steady = persistent action → AAVE
→ come = indignation → AAVE
📝 Examples
"She done finished" = completed · "I béen knew that" = I've known for a long time
"I finna leave" = about to leave · "He come telling me" = indignation
⚠️ Trap Unstressed been = StE present perfect. STRESSED béen = AAVE remote past. Prof compared béen to French passé surcomposé. Any of these markers = instant AAVE identification.
Puskás Ch.5 (AAVE)
M4 Negative Concord (Multiple Negation)
👁 What to Spot
Multiple negative elements: "I don't know nothing" or "Nobody don't like him."
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Neg. concord INCLUDING with subjects → AAVE (rare cross-linguistically!)
→ Neg. concord (objects/adverbs only) → IrE ScotE NigE + vernacular BrE
→ No negative concord → Standard varieties
📝 Examples
AAVE: "Nobody don't like him" (subject + verb both negative — AAVE-specific)
IrE/ScotE: "I didn't do nothing" (verb + object negative)
StE: "I didn't do anything"
⚠️ Trap Negative concord exists in MANY varieties — it's not unique to AAVE. What IS unique is negation on the subject ("Nobody don't..."). If you see NC but no subject negation, it could be several varieties. Use other features to narrow down.
Puskás Ch.5 (AAVE), Ch.2 (BrE vernaculars)
M5 Resumptive Pronouns
👁 What to Spot
Pronoun "copy" inside relative clause: "The man whom I saw him". The pronoun doubles the relative.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Resumptive pronouns → IrE ScotE (Celtic substrate) or NigE (W. African substrate)
Same pattern, different substrates — Prof: "Celtic and West African substrates — same pattern!"
📝 Examples
IrE: "The guests whom I invited them"
NigE: "The woman that I know her"
StE: "The guests whom I invited Ø" (gap, no pronoun)
⚠️ Trap Same surface feature in Celtic-substrate (IrE/ScotE) and W. African-substrate (NigE) varieties! Distinguish by other features: rhotic + schwa → IrE/ScotE; no schwa + syllable-timed → NigE.
Puskás Ch.3 (IrE/ScotE), Ch.6 (NigE)
M6 Discourse Particles
👁 What to Spot
Sentence-final particles: lah, mah, lor, ah, hor. These add pragmatic nuance.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Any discourse particle (lah, mah, lor...) → SingE / Singlish (UNIQUE)
lah = softener · mah = obvious · lor = resignation
📝 Examples
"Don't worry lah" = softening · "Like that lor" = resigned acceptance
"Of course mah" = it's obvious · "Can or not?" (A-not-A question, also SingE)
⚠️ Trap Particles = instant Singlish, no other variety has them. Substrate: Mandarin/Hokkien/Cantonese/Malay. Note: Singlish (vernacular) has particles; formal Singapore English may not. The variety shows diglossia.
Puskás Ch.8 (SingE/Singlish)
M7 Reduplication
👁 What to Spot
Words doubled for emphasis or meaning: "small small, now now, quick quick".
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Reduplication for intensification/iteration → NigE (West African substrate)
📝 Examples
"Come now now" = come immediately · "The road is small small" = very small / gradually small
"He was looking looking" = looking continuously
⚠️ Trap Reduplication = NigE marker. Don't confuse with SingE which doesn't reduplicate in the same way. W. African languages use reduplication productively — this transfers directly to NigE.
Puskás Ch.6 (NigE)
M8 Embedded Clause Inversion
👁 What to Spot
Subject-auxiliary inversion INSIDE embedded (subordinate) clauses — StE only allows it in main clauses.
🌳 Decision Tree
→ Inversion in embedded questions → IrE ScotE AAVE IndE
→ No inversion in embedded (standard order) → RP SAE
AAVE also has NO inversion in MAIN clause: "What is you saying?" = non-inverted!
📝 Examples
AAVE: "I asked her could I go" (inverted in embedded = AAVE)
IrE: "I wonder is she coming"
StE: "I asked her if I could go" (no inversion in embedded)
⚠️ Trap AAVE does the opposite of StE in BOTH directions: inverts in embedded (where StE doesn't) AND doesn't invert in main clause (where StE does). This double flip is diagnostic of AAVE.
